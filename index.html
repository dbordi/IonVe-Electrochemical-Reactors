The big picture

Electrochemical Reactors

Electrochemical reactors are devices that convert electrical energy into chemical energy, or the other way round, through controlled redox reactions at the cell level. They are the core hardware behind long-duration energy storage, green hydrogen production and industrial CO₂ conversion.

IonVe designs the core architecture of these reactors, from single cells to full industrial systems, aiming for the performance and cost levels needed to make large-scale deployment viable.

Current focus

Where We Focus

IonVe is currently focusing on its Redox Flow Battery (RFB) system, designed for industrial long-duration storage and now in prototype testing. We have demonstrated the proof of concept and are moving toward pilot plant development.

In parallel, we are starting to look at water electrolyzer architectures and CO₂ reduction reactors as the next step for our cell-engineering platform.

Technology primer

What is a flow battery?

Think of a liquid-filled tank that produces electricity as the fluid passes through an electrochemical cell.

  • More tank volume means more hours of autonomy
  • Unlike lithium-ion, the chemistry does not degrade over time
  • Capacity depends only on tank size
  • Non-flammable, inherently safe and fully recyclable

01

Long-duration storage is economically viable for industry today

Industrial sites with high demand charges and time-of-use contracts can already generate a positive business case for 4–12 hour storage. The barrier is the absence of an affordable, reliable product.

02

Lithium-ion is not cost-effective beyond 4–6 hours

At durations of 6 hours or more, the total cost of ownership of Li-ion over a 20–30 year industrial lifetime becomes significantly higher, mainly due to pack replacements.

03

Existing RFB systems remain too expensive

Current products remain constrained by high component count, machined graphite parts and custom engineering. Standardised, catalogue-style industrial products are still missing.

Architecture

Three architectural innovations for scalable electrochemical flow systems

IonVe introduces three architectural innovations for electrochemical flow systems, starting from redox flow batteries and extending naturally to other flow-based electrochemical reactors.

These innovations improve reactor architecture, module power density and system integration, creating a more efficient, lower cost and scalable platform that increases the competitiveness and economic value of the final product.

INNOVATION 01

Graphite-Free Reactors

We developed a new composite polymer material to replace expensive graphite components. It takes on multiple functions at once, so fewer parts are needed, and new design features become possible for higher efficiency.

Estimated cost reduction: 30–40% per cell vs. current RFB cells
INNOVATION 02

High-Power Ministack Module

A standardized ministack module triples power density, so the same system can use about three times fewer modules while improving efficiency, manufacturability and field maintenance.

Higher power density with fewer modules and simpler field assembly
INNOVATION 03

Modular Architecture

A modular architecture allows a higher level of scalability, flow distribution optimization, electrical reconfigurability and hot swap, simplifying power electronics and improving round-trip efficiency.

-20% instalation costs
FINAL PRODUCT

Integrated System Platform

The four innovations come together in a single product platform: cell, stack, hydraulics, power electronics and control, designed as one system for repeatable, series production.

Power range: 100 kW to 50 MW, same architecture, no re-engineering
